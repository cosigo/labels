MetaMask’s conduct in this matter was not a neutral act of user protection. It imposed a harmful designation, restricted normal owner-wallet contract interaction, blocked further meaningful chat assistance, and failed to identify a specific abusive function or clear remediation path. Instead of providing transparent review, MetaMask left correction trapped inside an opaque and unaccountable process while its label continued to carry real reputational and commercial consequences. When the same contract remained operable through Rabby, the practical conclusion became difficult to ignore: the impairment was not simply a contract-level failure, but a wallet-layer restriction imposed by MetaMask. That is not neutral risk guidance. It is discretionary gatekeeping with real market impact, and it deserves far more scrutiny than MetaMask has been willing to accept.
